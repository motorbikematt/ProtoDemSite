How the party
is organized

The Montgomery County Democratic Party operates through a precinct system — small geographic units that form the foundation of every election. Each precinct elects a captain who connects neighbors to the broader party, coordinates volunteers, and ensures every voice is heard. This site archives party proceedings so participation isn't limited to who can attend in person.

01
Find your precinct

Montgomery County is divided into 141 precincts. Your precinct determines your local representatives, your captain, and your polling location.

02
Meet your captain

Each precinct elects a captain to serve as the bridge between neighbors and the party. Captains organize locally, knock doors, and make sure every vote is counted.

03
Attend or watch

Party meetings are open. Central Committee convenes quarterly. All sessions are recorded, transcribed, and published here so you're never out of the loop.
