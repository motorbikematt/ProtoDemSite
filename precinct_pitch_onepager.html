Pitch draft — April 2026

Reconnecting voters
to the party machine
they don't know they have.

A civic transparency and local engagement platform that makes Democratic Party structure legible, participatory, and useful — starting in Montgomery County, Ohio.

The problem

Structural opacity

Most Democratic voters don't know their precinct captain exists.

The party's most local layer — the elected precinct representative — is functionally invisible. Captains are often appointed rather than elected, uncontested, and unreachable. The machinery meant to represent voters represents itself instead.

Participation friction

Civic life has become too laborious to compete with daily survival.

Two-hour council meetings, opaque agendas, and fragmented information across platforms means the cognitive cost of staying engaged now exceeds what most working people can sustain. Disengagement is a rational response to a broken interface.

The context: Montgomery County, Ohio

530K county residents across Dayton metro
100+ precincts across Dayton, Kettering, Centerville & townships
1 active local Democratic club as beachhead partner

The solution

Core product

A precinct-level civic map with LLM-summarized official content and a direct participation pathway.

Citizens select their address and see: who their precinct captain is, when they were last elected vs. appointed, what their party has actually voted on, and what their city council or school board covered at its last meeting — summarized from official agendas and minutes, not curated commentary. A single clear action follows each summary: contact your rep, attend the next meeting, sign up to run.

How it works

01

Citizen enters address. Map resolves their precinct, ward, school district, and city council seat.

02

Official documents — agendas, minutes, blotters — are ingested and summarized by LLM into plain-language digests.

03

Each digest surfaces one action: reach your rep, attend, respond to a pulse survey, or share.

04

Participation data aggregates for the party and small governments as anonymous sentiment intelligence.

Revenue model (hypotheses)

Primary: party & club subscriptions

County parties, Democratic clubs, and state party chapters pay a modest SaaS subscription for a constituent engagement dashboard — participation rates, precinct health scores, and meeting digest delivery to members. SDDC as founding customer.

Secondary: small government sentiment

City councils, school boards, and township trustees pay for continuous voice-of-constituent surveys tied to specific agenda items — replacing public comment theater with structured, anonymous input before and after each meeting.

The ask

Pre-seed experiment,
one county, one cycle.

Funding to build and validate the Montgomery County MVP: precinct map, document ingestion pipeline, LLM summarization layer, and SDDC-facing dashboard. Measurable outcome: demonstrable increase in precinct captain contact rates and club membership within one political cycle.
